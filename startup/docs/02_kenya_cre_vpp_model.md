# VPP for Kenyan Commercial Real Estate (CRE)

In the Kenyan CRE sector, a VPP would transform business parks, malls, and warehouses into "energy hubs" that generate, store, and trade power to stabilize the national grid.

## Restructured VPP Model for Kenyan CRE

### Asset Aggregation
Links:
- rooftop solar PV on malls/offices
- battery banks in buildings
- EV charging stations in business parks

### Grid Services
During national peak demand (typically **6:00 PM – 10:00 PM**), VPP discharges stored energy from commercial batteries into the grid to prevent blackouts.

### Internal Trading
Tenants can buy excess solar energy from the building’s main VPP at lower-than-standard tariffs, managed via smart prepaid meters.

## Can CRE Energy Help the Grid?
Yes—potential is significant.

### Scale indicators (as captured in the notes)
- Captive solar capacity (incl. CRE/industrial): **~300.5 MW (mid-2025)** (~8% of national installed capacity).
- Solar generation: **~473 GWh/year**.
- C&I segment growth: **~13% annually**.

### Potential impacts
- **Peak mitigation:** commercial solar aligns with daytime peaks; reduces strain and improves reliability.
- **Cost:** aggregated VPP power estimated cheaper than thermal peakers (as framed in the notes).
- **Business case:** paybacks cited at **2–4 years** for CRE systems (from the notes).
