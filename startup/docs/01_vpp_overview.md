# Virtual Power Plant (VPP) — Overview

A Virtual Power Plant (VPP) is a cloud-based network that links multiple small-scale, decentralized energy resources into a single, coordinated power source. Instead of one massive central generator, it uses thousands of smaller assets—like home batteries, rooftop solar panels, and electric vehicles—to act as one giant battery or generator for the electrical grid.

## How It Works
A VPP operates through a cycle of monitoring, coordination, and dispatching:

### Aggregation
Advanced software connects various distributed energy resources (DERs) into a unified digital system (e.g., batteries, solar PV, smart thermostats, EV chargers).

### Real-Time Monitoring
The central platform constantly tracks grid demand, weather forecasts, and electricity prices.

### Intelligent Dispatching
- **High demand:** VPP signals participating devices to discharge energy / reduce load.
- **Low demand:** VPP signals devices to charge / absorb surplus generation.

### Financial Settlement
Participants are compensated via payments, bill credits, or equipment discounts.

## Why They Matter
- **Grid stability:** fast response to prevent blackouts during peaks.
- **Cleaner energy:** can replace fossil peaker plants.
- **Cost efficiency:** uses existing assets vs building new infrastructure.
